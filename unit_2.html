Unit 2: Criminological Theories

Criminologists have produced theoretical explanations of why people commit crime - this throws up a number of questions:

• Which are the most useful? 

• Are these theories relevant to all types of crime?

• How can these theories be applied to real-life crimes?

Students will apply their understanding of the public perceptions of crime and campaigns for change studied in Unit 1, with criminological theories, and will examine how both are used to set policy.
